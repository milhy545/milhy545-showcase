3R Principle

Re-fix. Reuse. Recycle. Not a slogan, not a retro aesthetic, and not an excuse to avoid upgrades. A discipline for extracting real value from imperfect systems.

Re-fix

Repair the thing you already have, understand its failure mode, and gain operational knowledge in the process.

Reuse

Deploy what still works. Old hardware and old ideas both deserve another chance if the constraint can be made productive.

Recycle

Turn discarded material into a new substrate. In software, this also means preserving useful concepts from dead branches and prototypes.

Why it belongs here

This philosophy explains why a museum makes sense at all. Old machines, old branches, and older ideas are not automatically junk. Some become infrastructure. Some become lessons. Either way, they deserve to be catalogued instead of forgotten.
